# Energy System Analysis using GAMS

## 1) Project Overview

This repository contains a collection of energy system modelling and optimization projects completed during my Master's studies in Mechanical Engineering (Specialization: Sustainable Energy Systems) at Ruhr University Bochum, Germany.
The projects focus on developing mathematical optimization models to analyse and optimize energy systems under different technical and economic scenarios. The work includes power plant scheduling, investment planning, renewable energy integration, and system-wide optimization using the General Algebraic Modeling System (GAMS).
These projects demonstrate my ability to formulate engineering problems as optimization models, analyse complex energy systems, and derive technically and economically sound solutions.

## 2) Project Objectives

The main objectives of this project are to:

- Develop optimization models for energy system planning and operation.
- Evaluate different energy generation and investment scenarios.
- Analyse the impact of renewable energy integration.
- Minimize total system costs while satisfying technical constraints.
- Improve understanding of mathematical modelling techniques used in modern energy systems.
- Apply engineering decision-making based on optimization results.

## 4) Methodology

The project follows a structured engineering modelling approach:

1. Define the energy system and decision variables.
2. Formulate technical and operational constraints.
3. Define the optimization objective (typically cost minimization).
4. Implement mathematical models using GAMS.
5. Execute simulations under different scenarios.
6. Analyse optimization outputs.
7. Compare different operational and investment strategies.
8. Document engineering conclusions and recommendations.
